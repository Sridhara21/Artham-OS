## 1. System Topology & Operational Layers

ARTHAM OS X is structured as a six-layer intelligence operating system designed to observe, reason, simulate, recommend, replay, and scale global physical telemetry into sovereign economic understanding.

```
       +-------------------------------------------------------+
       |             ARTHAM OS X SYSTEM LAYER STACK            |
       +-------------------------------------------------------+
       | Layer 6: GLOBALIZE -> ARTHAM EARTH™ (3D Global flows) |
       | Layer 5: LEARN     -> CHRONOS™ (Economic replay logs) |
       | Layer 4: RECOMMEND -> ECONOMIC AUTOPILOT™ (Mitigation)|
       | Layer 3: PREDICT   -> SCENARIO LAB™ (Simulation engine)|
       | Layer 2: REASON    -> ARTHAM PRIME™ (Causal Graphs)   |
       | Layer 1: OBSERVE   -> ARTHAM TWIN™ (Spatial Digital)  |
       +-------------------------------------------------------+
```

### Layer 1: OBSERVE — ARTHAM TWIN™
*   **Purpose**: Multi-scale spatial digital twin representing physical cargo movements in real-time.
*   **Mechanics**: Maps road FASTag telemetry, port manifests, and railway scheduling sensors onto an abstract geographical overlay.
*   **Resolution Scope**: Tracks variables from the national corridor scale down to individual container terminal gates.

### Layer 2: REASON — ARTHAM PRIME™
*   **Purpose**: Causal reasoning engine linking local physical cargo delays to national inflation and financial yields.
*   **Mechanics**: Generates Directed Acyclic Causal Graphs (DAGs) in response to natural language queries. Citations are verified using participating agent audits and empirical data registries.

### Layer 3: PREDICT — SCENARIO LAB™
*   **Purpose**: Sovereign simulation sandbox for economic and logistical stress-testing.
*   **Mechanics**: Models the systemic ripple effects of shock variables (e.g., $150 Brent Crude, monsoon drops, or major port shutdowns). Estimates expected GDP and CPI projections across three-tier bounds.

### Layer 4: RECOMMEND — ECONOMIC AUTOPILOT™
*   **Purpose**: Decision intelligence layer providing active logistical overrides and mitigation blueprints.
*   **Mechanics**: Suggests routing bypasses, container shifts, and cargo prioritization strategies. Quantifies cost, carbon, transit duration, and GDP impact offsets.

### Layer 5: LEARN — CHRONOS™
*   **Purpose**: Historical economic replay engine.
*   **Mechanics**: Rewinds, pauses, and re-runs past disruptions (e.g., the 2021 Suez Canal blockage, 2024 Red Sea crisis, or historical monsoonal failures) to analyze failures and benchmark policy responses.

### Layer 6: GLOBALIZE — ARTHAM EARTH™
*   **Purpose**: Planetary logistics observatory.
*   **Mechanics**: Renders a rotating 3D globe plotting maritime shipping corridors, global rail lines, and terminal dwell queues. Acts as the unified visual dashboard for global trade flows.

## 3. Proprietary Economic Indices

ARTHAM OS X uses seven proprietary indices to translate physical cargo logs into economic forecasts.

### I. FreightGDP™ ($F_{GDP}$)
*   **Formula**:
    $$F_{GDP} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \left( \frac{V_{i, t}}{V_{i, \text{base}}} \times \omega_i \times \alpha_i \right)$$
    Where $V_{i, t}$ is the daily tonnage of commodity $i$, $V_{i, \text{base}}$ is the 3-year seasonal average, $\omega_i$ represents the sectoral GDP input factor, and $\alpha_i$ is the velocity coefficient.
*   **Methodology**: Measures real-time physical industrial momentum using railway FOIS and highway Fastag toll registries.
*   **Value**: Provides a leading indicator for Q-o-Q industrial GDP growth.

### II. Economic Momentum Index™ ($EMI$)
*   **Formula**:
    $$EMI_t = \gamma_1 \cdot \Delta F_{GDP} + \gamma_2 \cdot \Delta TPI - \gamma_3 \cdot CSI$$
    Where $\Delta F_{GDP}$ is the 10-day rate of change in FreightGDP, $\Delta TPI$ is the Trade Pulse Index shift, $CSI$ is the national Corridor Stress Index average, and $\gamma$ represent sensitivity weights.
*   **Methodology**: Integrates velocity, trade, and stress metrics into a single score of national economic momentum.
*   **Value**: Serves as a primary reference indicator for central bank monetary policy adjustments.

### III. Corridor Stress Index™ ($CSI$)
*   **Formula**:
    $$CSI_k = \left( 1 - \frac{S_t}{S_d} \right) \cdot w_s + \left( \frac{D_t}{D_c} \right) \cdot w_d$$
    Where $S_t$ is actual segment speed, $S_d$ is design speed, $D_t$ is real-time vehicle density, and $D_c$ is route capacity.
*   **Methodology**: Computes congestion and delay ratios along main DFC rail and National Highway corridors.
*   **Value**: Informs infrastructure investment and dynamic routing overrides.

### IV. Supply Chain Health Score™ ($SCHS$)
*   **Formula**:
    $$SCHS = 100 \times \exp \left( -\beta \cdot \sigma^2_T \right) \times \left( 1 - \frac{D_{\text{port}} + D_{\text{wh}}}{D_{\text{base}}} \right)$$
    Where $\sigma^2_T$ is the variance in route transit times, $D$ represent port and warehouse dwell times, and $\beta$ is a decay coefficient.
*   **Methodology**: Evaluates supply chain predictability by tracking lead times and dwell latencies.
*   **Value**: Helps manufacturers optimize buffer stocks and adjust production planning.

### V. Trade Pulse Index™ ($TPI$)
*   **Formula**:
    $$TPI = \frac{\Phi_{\text{exp}} + \Phi_{\text{imp}}}{\Phi_{\text{base}}} \times \left( 1 - \bar{D}_{\text{customs}} \right)$$
    Where $\Phi$ is daily trade values, and $\bar{D}_{\text{customs}}$ represents customs processing delays.
*   **Methodology**: Measures international trade velocity at major ports (JNPT, Mundra, Chennai) and air hubs.
*   **Value**: Identifies bottlenecks in trade corridors and predicts trade balance shifts.

### VI. Infrastructure Utilization Index™ ($IUI$)
*   **Formula**:
    $$IUI = \frac{\sum \text{Used Capacity}}{\sum \text{Design Capacity}} \times 100$$
    Where used capacity incorporates container movements, wagon utilization, and warehouse volume occupancy.
*   **Methodology**: Aggregates capacity utilization metrics across logistics nodes.
*   **Value**: Informs capital expenditure allocations and identifies underutilized infrastructure.

### VII. Commodity Velocity Score™ ($CVS$)
*   **Formula**:
    $$CVS_c = \frac{\text{Distance}_{\text{km}}}{\text{Transit Time}_{\text{hrs}}} \times \mu_c$$
    Where $\mu_c$ is the economic priority multiplier for a given commodity class $c$ (e.g., Coal has a higher priority multiplier during energy shortages).
*   **Methodology**: Measures the transit speed of primary commodities through logistics networks.
*   **Value**: Alerts policymakers to supply bottlenecks for critical industrial resources.

## 5. System AI & Data Architecture

To support these real-time reasoning and simulation layers, ARTHAM OS X operates on a unified data and AI stack.

```
       +-------------------------------------------------------+
       |               ARTHAM OS X INTEL STACK                 |
       +-------------------------------------------------------+
       | AI: Agent Council (Consensus & Causal Reasoners)      |
       |     Simulation & Forecast Engines (IPCC & Hedonic)    |
       +-------------------------------------------------------+
       | DATA FUSION: ClickHouse Time-Series Engine            |
       |              Neo4j Causal Graph Database              |
       +-------------------------------------------------------+
       | STREAM INGEST: Kafka Clusters & Spark Stream Cleaners |
       +-------------------------------------------------------+
       | TELEMETRY: FOIS, PCS Ports, FASTag APIs, Sentinel Sats|
       +-------------------------------------------------------+
```

### Data Pipelines & Storage
*   **Data Fusion Engine**: Combines FASTag road logs, rail scheduling logs, port community system logs, satellite images, and weather telemetry.
*   **Streaming & Processing**: Apache Kafka streams incoming data to Apache Spark nodes, which filter out noise and identify outliers.
*   **Database Stack**:
    *   *ClickHouse*: Stores high-speed time-series statistics.
    *   *Neo4j*: Maps causal connections and dependency paths.

### AI Reasoning Stack
*   **Foundation Models**: Employs Gemini 2.0 to convert unstructured inputs (e.g., satellite images, news alerts) into structured causal graph nodes.
*   **Reasoning DAGs**: Neo4j paths are evaluated using agent consensus matrices to verify causal integrity.
*   **Simulation Engine**: Combines logistics cost models, IPCC emission formulas, and hedonic pricing models to calculate simulated outcomes.
